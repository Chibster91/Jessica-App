Design notes

  • Sub-tab control. Pill segmented control inside --surface container with the active pill in --accent. Distinct from the bottom nav and from the existing in-screen text tabs (used in Library) so users can read it as "I'm picking a view of one screen" rather than navigating away.
  • Current. Hero ring uses a conic-gradient progress mask (no SVG) — reuses Jessica's purple at the same chroma. Start/Goal flank it as quiet metadata. Progress + Time bars borrow the calorie-bar style from the home screen so the visual vocabulary stays consistent. The little segment indicator (⬛⬛⬛⬜⬜) reads as "completion tier" the way the history rows show streak quality.
  • Graph. Range pills are the same pill style as the rest of the system. SVG line chart uses --accent for the actual series, plus dashed mint/amber lines for goal-pace and trend, picked from the macro palette so we don't introduce new colors. Y/X labels in mono. The selected-point readout (date · weight · BMI · expected date) sits above the chart instead of overlapping it like the reference.
  • History. Single column of hairline-separated rows (no row backgrounds — that's our convention). The five-segment bar reuses macro colors as a quality tier (red = bad, amber = neutral, green = on-track). Date column is mono, day-of-week + clock muted.
  • What I dropped from the references. The blue chrome, status bar, in-app banner ad, and the icon-glyph top tab strip — not part of our system. The teal background gradient on the "current" screen was replaced with our flat --surface panel. The "Enter Weight" CTA is the primary purple button instead of a grey pill.

Locked decisions

  • Units default to lb.
  • BMI category badge stays amber (--macro-fat).
  • Five-segment quality bar stays in History rows.
  • Graph supports pinch-to-zoom; the knob beside the range pills mirrors the current zoom level (drag to zoom on desktop, pinch on touch). Range pills snap to preset windows.
